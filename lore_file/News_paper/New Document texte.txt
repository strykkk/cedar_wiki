Unity City:

The sky was overcast, and light snow was falling. The "Cosmodrome Tactical Unit" marched out in three neat rows from a rusty iron gate, their bayonets gleaming coldly on their shoulders. Captain Julius surveyed everything with a stern gaze—from the overcast sky to the passersby who were taking cover nearby before getting into the back of a sidecar motorcycle.

As a member of the most important social class in the noble republic of Ruvila, the knightly class Julius served in the United Territory police force, which was characterized by a thoroughly Phalansterian pragmatism, Julius held the position of squad leader. Her seriousness and selflessness earned her both respect and fear from her colleagues. A subtle blend of haughty chivalry and the suffocating collectivism within the Iron Curtain clung to her. Julias never neglected her military bearing, always carrying a saber with a gilded hilt and a full set of daggers, polishing the embroidered silver threads on her collar and sleeve insignia until they gleamed. These gleaming ornaments might attract fire during operations, but they also made her easily intimidating—some colleagues would even scream at the mere sight of her.

This 19-year-old female knight came from a "two-foot-tall" family.

"two-foot-tall" families—in the Republic of Luvila, this refers to impoverished noble families who have lost all their possessions. Therefore, she had reason to be resentful of her current job: driving away angry, hungry scavengers across a vast wasteland littered with rocket fuel contamination, who could devour landed pods like termites. Julius's official pay was 2,500 Solidarity Credits per month—equivalent to about 9.3 Welsh pounds at the practically nonexistent official exchange rate—and it was often delayed by a month or two.

“Of course, prices are high in Unity City, and I also need to buy a lot of books, so I have to cut back on my food and clothing and live frugally.” When questioned by a reporter about how he could make a living with such a low legal income, Julius said nonchalantly, “But other units are in even more difficulty, so I have nothing to complain about.”

A Western-like scenario plays out almost weekly in the Union. Since 1993, Earth's annual tonnage into orbit has decreased by 77%, and at the other end of this fragile, slender space highway lies the only trace of modern civilization on Knox—crude Union-style consumer goods, massive, gloomy factories, and unsymmetrical dimethylhydrazine propellant flowing into waterways, bubbling in puddles at every landing site, causing deformities and premature births in the surrounding infants. Julius is acutely aware of the dangers of these byproducts of civilization—sometimes she has to cover her ornate military uniform with a rubber hazmat suit and fasten a skull-like gas mask while chasing scavengers.

“Once you get used to it, you can run and shoot,” Julius said, “though it’s difficult.” As she spoke, she was removing her disposable protective suit so the fire department could collect, clean, and reuse it. And the scavengers vying for the landing site had more than just that…
