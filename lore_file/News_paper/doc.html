Unity City:
The sky was overcast, and light snow was falling. The "Cosmodrome Tactical Unit" marched out in three neat rows from a rusty iron gate, their bayonets gleaming coldly on their shoulders. Captain Julius surveyed everything with a stern gaze—from the overcast sky to the passersby who were taking cover nearby before getting into the back of a sidecar motorcycle.

As a member of the most important social class in the noble republic of Ruvila, the knightly class Julius served in the United Territory police force, which was characterized by a thoroughly Phalansterian pragmatism, Julius held the position of squad leader. Her seriousness and selflessness earned her both respect and fear from her colleagues. A subtle blend of haughty chivalry and the suffocating collectivism within the Iron Curtain clung to her. Julius never neglected her military bearing, always carrying a saber with a gilded hilt and a full set of daggers, polishing the embroidered silver threads on her collar and sleeve insignia until they gleamed. These gleaming ornaments might attract fire during operations, but they also made her easily intimidating—some colleagues would even scream at the mere sight of her.

This 19-year-old female knight came from a "two-foot-tall" family.

"two-foot-tall" families—in the Republic of Ruvila refers to impoverished noble families who have lost all their possessions. Therefore, she had reason to be resentful of her current job: driving away angry, hungry scavengers across a vast wasteland littered with rocket fuel contamination, scavengers could devour landed pods like termites. Julius's official pay was 2,500 union ruble per month—equivalent to about 9.3 Vespuccian pounds at the practically nonexistent official exchange rate—and it was often delayed by a month or two.

A Western-like scenario plays out almost weekly in the Union. Since 1993, Earth's annual tonnage into orbit has decreased by 77%, and at the other end of this fragile, slender space highway lies the only trace of modern civilization on Nox crude Union-style consumer goods, massive, gloomy factories, and unsymmetrical dimethylhydrazine propellant flowing into waterways, bubbling in puddles at every landing site, causing deformities and premature births in the surrounding infants. Julius is acutely aware of the dangers of these byproducts of civilization—sometimes she has to cover her ornate military uniform with a rubber hazmat suit and fasten a skull-like gas mask while chasing scavengers.

“Once you get used to it, you can run and shoot,” Julius said, “though it’s difficult.” As she spoke, she was removing her disposable hazmat suit so the fire department could collect, clean, and reuse it. And the scavengers vying for the landing site had more than just that…

The landing capsule's cargo: Scavengers would dismantle any valuable parts from the landing capsule itself to trade or repair their own old equipment. Most of the time, the Cosmodrome Tactical Unit didn't interfere with the scavengers who recycled scrap equipment. However, the battle for the junk and cargo intensified; in Julius's words, "one piece of junk had three groups eyeing it." The Cosmodrome Tactical Unit, with only a hundred or so personnel, had to mobilize its entire force on every landing recovery day. Even so, a considerable amount of important cargo launched from Earth was still stolen and ended up on the black market.

Every morning in Unity City, hurried crowds fill the streets shrouded in a bluish mist. This mist comes from pollutants emitted by the United Central Petrochemical Complex and the "Spark" Joint Nitrogenation Plant, both employing nearly ten thousand workers. Standing among the commuters and observing the street scene, anyone would feel that the Union still seems to exist in this small corner of civilization: the dull streets are devoid of neon lights and commercial advertisements, the roadside is covered with mimeographed posters, and people place their lunchboxes in the steam cabinets of the factory canteens, carrying hemp rope net bags to queue for food. In the kindergartens and schools attached to the factories and government agencies, mixed-race children chase and play wildly, making "cannonballs" in mud puddles to bombard the windows of teachers they dislike. It is a time capsule.

Forty-nine years after Sputnik 1 ignited a space frenzy, 1.2 million Earthlings a significant decrease from the 1990 figure—and 700,000 Noxian holding "Union Territory Internal Passports" resided in the United Territory. All of them were either forced or voluntarily dependent on the United Territory Under a bureaucratic system, they lived off the salaries of "public enterprises".

According to the concept of "Interstellar Phalanstism," this kind of life would be a "bright future" for all humanity and the Noxian people. However, even in the queues at state-run grocery stores, we could see customers automatically forming two lines, one of which consisted only of Nox people—many of whom could only buy negotiated goods, that is, goods that required cash but not ration coupons. This reminded me of my years living in the Bronx.

Blue-collar workers on fixed wages would purchase a fixed amount of food and consumer goods with vouchers at public stores (including joint grain stores, supply and marketing cooperatives, and department stores). Outside Unity City, numerous collective farms and ranches supplied the city with grain, livestock, and a limited variety of fruit in exchange for fuel, consumer goods, and crude, cumbersome tools manufactured by the city.
The Allied powers, in their struggle for influence over Nox, actively participated in establishing this planned system that left little room for independent operators. Meanwhile, the Allied leaders paid farmers in Unity Rubles according to the official prices outlined in the "procurement plan."

But with the collapse of the Iron Curtain, the grand ambition of conquering the universe has become a hangover-induced dream, forced to give way to the credit card bills in the mailbox, the United Territory has undertaken a series of economic liberalization reforms, jeans, game consoles, and music tapes rain down from the sky. Of course, these things couldn't be bought with the Unity Rubles which was commonly known as "great unity (Veliedi-n)." They required foreign exchange, namely the Vespuccian Pounds or even the Union Rubles. This currency, which had lost its country, miraculously maintained its past credit on the planet Nox, and even maintained a fixed exchange rate against the Vespuccian pounds.

(5) "What's the difference between a person without dreams and Kirby?"

Only when pursuing the fruits of consumerism do Earthlings and Noxian truly achieve equality, regardless of the means employed. This is precisely the case with Miss Ishtar's video store. She doesn't always wear pink dresses, but rather changes into the latest Akitsu anime character costumes from time to time to show her rebellion against Ruvilian's traditions. She uses the Akitsu-derived neologism "cosplay" to describe her attire—and swears that all her merchandise is compliant with regulations. By any standard, buying hobby items symbolizing "dreams" here is not cheap. "What's the difference between a person without dreams and Snorlax!" Miss Ishtar says, sorting through obviously pirated comic books. "But maintaining dreams requires more than just a 'soul,' there are many other things..."

Towards interstellar civilization
On June 29, 1971, two Soyuz astronauts became the first to land on Nox, prepared to survive independently on the planet for several years, perhaps even their entire lives, without ever returning to Earth. Unexpectedly, the Nox inhabitants showed no suspicion or hostility towards the extraterrestrial visitors; on the contrary, they treated them with exceptional hospitality and warmly welcomed more Earthlings to visit.
The fact that the Union was the first to make friends with extraterrestrials most significantly demonstrates its space superiority. Under the Alliance's leadership and with the participation of countries worldwide, crucial space infrastructure, including momentum exchange ropes, was established.
Its completion marked a significant step forward for humanity and the Noxians towards a shared future in a space city. Unity City also successfully broke ground in 1975, finally bringing the conditions for large-scale communication between the two planets to mature.
The successful landing on another habitable planet has profoundly influenced human thought. Against the backdrop of large-scale space cooperation, the Union proposed "Interstellar phalanstery," arguing that humanity is irreversibly moving towards interstellar civilization, and that the space program, requiring concentrated resources and global collaboration,and will promote the integration of nations and peoples into a unified whole, ultimately achieving a harmonious society.
NOX and EGO

EGO is a supernatural ability unique to Ruvilian women, spontaneously unleashed under the influence of strong emotions.

EGO will exhibit different effects depending on the user's different emotions.
Including but not limited to:
Enhanced limb abilities, control of external temperature, manipulation of fluids, special senses, interference with others' minds, healing abilities, etc.
However, the power of most individuals' EGO is very limited, insufficient to give women a dominant position in Nox society, and naturally cannot be compared with the power of Earth's technology.
