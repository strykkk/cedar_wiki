Personal History

       Lisa serves as a reconnaissance soldier within the 8th Squad of the United Development Territory Security Bureau. At just sixteen years of age, she is the squad's youngest member. By the 8th Squad's past standards, Lisa has not been fully trained in the conduct and discipline expected of a special forces operative. She is prone to shirking duties, fond of napping, takes considerably longer afternoon naps than her comrades and has a particular penchant for vanishing without a trace under the guise of conducting stealth training.

       Elena's team was severely depleted, making her reliance on Lisa's reconnaissance talents all the more critical. Lisa possessed an innate, acute spatial awareness: even while dozing or with her eyes closed, she could accurately perceive the movements of personnel and vehicles around her. She could even discern whether survivors lay beneath collapsed rubble. However, excessive noise renders Lisa's abilities ineffective. Elena therefore surmised that this was actually a product of exceptionally acute hearing combined with powerful associative reasoning.

       In other words, Lisa actually possesses a remarkably sharp mind, yet she employs only half of it to complete her tasks, reserving the other half for idleness. Lisa invariably finds the routine training exercises far too straightforward, with enemy behaviour being overly predictable. She could master them with her eyes closed, which only serves to sap her motivation.

       As a soldier, Lisa had little enthusiasm for violence. Or rather, given the choice, she preferred not to eliminate or capture enemies outright, but rather to toy with her subjects of observation. She delighted in playing minor pranks upon them, introducing a touch of unpredictability into their overly regimented routines, thereby adding a measure of variety to her own monotonous reconnaissance duties. This habit of underestimating adversaries and courting danger frequently drew Elena's rebuke, for Elena knew far more perilous foes lurked in the shadows. Yet since Lisa had never once failed in her mission, such reprimands proved largely ineffective.

       Lisa's stealth abilities were unanimously acknowledged by her teammates, including Karin. Elena also admitted that if Lisa chose to remain undetected, she could conceal herself indefinitely. Not only were humanoid creatures difficult to detect, but even insects and animals driven by primal instincts could be deceived by her. Thus, one might often witness small creatures like squirrels or hares being gently cradled and handled by her. Lisa, who harboured no desire to harm humans, naturally harboured none towards animals either. After playing with these little creatures for a while, she would release them all.

       Lisa, possessing extraordinary skills, was often mistaken for a Noxian with only distant blood ties, yet she was in fact a pure-blooded Terran. Consequently, Elena frequently cited her as proof that "EGO" held no mystical power. Both her parents hailed from Scandinavia and adopted a remarkably hands-off approach to her upbringing. In the vast, sparsely populated United Territories, Lisa swiftly became an untamed child beyond anyone's control, her mischief skills so formidable that all teachers avoided her. Aside from Elena recognising her talents, no school or institution was willing to take her in.

       Lisa hadn't yet developed a fully formed sense of justice or honour. Her sense of belonging to the 8th Squad was exceptionally strong, for it offered greater freedom than school and allowed her to roam freely across the United Territories, taking in all manner of novel sights. Yet after two years of service, Lisa felt the United Territories held no further wonders for her. When might Elena take her to explore a wider world? Personal Report

       Lisa is a scout in the 8th Squad. Because of her mischievousness, tendency to talk nonsense, frequent slacking off and dozing off, and her penchant for disappearing under the guise of "infiltration training," she's Elena's most troublesome subordinate. Lisa's parents are both well-educated intellectuals, but they adopted a "laissez-faire"(let do) approach to her upbringing. At school, Lisa's pranks were so severe that all the teachers avoided her, so no other unit wanted to hire her. Only Elena recognized her talent and saw it could fill the gaps in the 8th Squad's roster, and Lisa felt a strong sense of belonging to the 8th Squad here, she wasn't required to sit cooped up in the classroom, but could frequently run around the United Territory, enjoying the various novel outdoor sights.

       Lisa's stealth abilities are unanimously recognized; if she doesn't want to be found, she can almost remain hidden forever. She also possesses an exceptionally strong sense of spatial awareness; even while dozing off with her eyes closed, she can accurately detect the movement of surrounding creatures and vehicles, allowing her to escape in time if Elena tries to capture her.

       Lisa's remarkable intuition fails when the noise is too loud, so Elena deduces that this is a product of a combination of extremely keen hearing and associative abilities—in other words, Lisa actually has a fairly intelligent mind, but she only uses half of it to complete the task, while using the other half to slack off. Lisa always felt that the daily training exercises were too easy, and the enemies they faced behaved in a predictable way. They could be understood even with their eyes closed, which made her lose motivation.

       If she could act on her own, she preferred to tease her targets, playing little pranks on them to add some spice to her life. The various animals she encountered during her missions were also inevitably the subject of her teasing; it was common to see squirrels or rabbits held lightly in her arms—Lisa, who had no desire to harm humans, naturally wouldn't harm animals either. After playing with these little creatures for a while, she would release them all back into the wild.

       Elena frequently reprimanded Lisa for underestimating the enemy, always emphasizing the existence of far greater threats. However, since Lisa had never failed, these reprimands were largely ineffective. After two years of service, Lisa felt the United Territory had lost its novelty. When would Elena take her to see the wider world and witness those "far greater threats"?
