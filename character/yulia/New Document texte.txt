到底是什么人能够节制如同秃鹫般饥不择食的拾荒者？——这是警卫局特别是警卫机动队一直在努力侦办的首要问题，然而拾荒者的组织并不那么容易打入，这些聚散无常的群落中存在着一些联系紧密的小团体，拥卫着一个只有诨名传世的领袖。小团体利用大群拾荒者作为掩护，出现得刁钻，消失得狡猾，只取走最值钱的货物和零件，甚至还有自己的车辆。因此警卫局认为他们的头目大概率是地球出身，受过高等教育的人。

尤利娅就是这样一个外号"邮差"的拾荒者头目，可她偏偏又不是地球人，而且只有共和国的高小文化水平，这个貌似稚嫩柔弱的少女能成为最有威望的拾荒者头目，完全是依靠天才般的智慧。


看不出什么书卷气的尤利娅有着极强的学习能力，凭借四处搜刮的各种一鳞半爪的资料，就自己摸索出了许多实用的土办法，用来拆解和拼凑各种部件，洞悉物品的真正价值。同样，她的武器——RPG-7也是一种射击技巧很难掌握的高手向武器，在大多数人手里只能像烟花一样四处乱飞，而在她手里则可以做到精确射击。像AGS17榴弹发射器这样的复杂东西更是只有她才玩得转。由于周围的绝大多数人都是头脑简单，鼠目寸光的家伙，尤利娅也难免经常会有智力上的优越感。


尤利娅的外号略有偏差地指出了她的出身：她曾经是一位邮局的职员，不过并非是跑腿送信的。太空竞赛时期的联合领有非常宏大的农业开发和工业建设计划，这其中就包括曾经试图建立一个遍及诺克斯已知大陆的邮政系统——现在这个系统只在联合领居民点和共和国的一些大城市有残留网点，而且职员大多数都是地球人，不过尤利娅和许多诺克斯人一样，曾为这一系统服务。

早在尤利娅小学三年级的时候，她的老师就已经断言她是自己教过最出众的学生。然而在高小毕业之后，无论骑士的寄宿学校还是执政府文教部办的中学都没有她的名额——因为名额都被各种各样有"资格"的人预定了。以她的学历，所能谋取的最体面的差事就是在联合领当个邮局的职员。

在邮局，她经手了无数封信件和明信片，通过邮票和明信片上的图样，她见识了地球上的高山大河，冰原峡谷，珍禽异兽，当然还有巨轮，水坝和摩天大楼。尤利娅的天赋依然闪耀：她往往看一眼就能够大概推理出邮票上那些复杂的机械装置有什么用处，又是靠什么原理运作的。

邮局还给了她许多阅读书刊杂志的机会，在学习知识之余也让她看出了联盟已经气息奄奄，到时候邮局必然要裁减，而自己的饭碗也正摇摇欲坠。但是尤利娅没能想到的是，她的职业生涯却先于邮局结束。


在解体之后的混乱时期，一伙由波拉率领的流民冲进了了邮局意图抢劫。可是此时的邮局已经没有什么现金和值钱的东西了，于是波拉转而抓了尤利娅作为人质，想要联合领交付赎金，因为当时尤利娅戴着制帽，被当成了地球人。

在弄清楚尤利娅的真实身份之后，波拉试图学着黑帮老大的样子，想要强迫这位俘虏拜自己为"教母"，成为自己死心塌地的跟班，但后来却发生了许多波拉稀里糊涂说不清楚而尤利娅不屑于提及的事情，总之结果是空有蛮力的波拉反而成了"教女"，而尤利娅从俘虏身份咸鱼翻身，成为团伙领袖。她完全清楚明火执仗地抢劫绑票肯定要被警卫局杀鸡儆猴，所以尤利娅开创了降落场拾荒这一行业——就算不是首创者，至少也是先驱之一。

由于联合领的收缩和共和国的动荡，大量低素质从业人员的涌入让蓝海市场很快成为红海。拾荒者成群结队，但尤利娅的地位却不降反升。饿急眼了的愣头青一看到降落舱的伞花，就飞蛾扑火般冲向警卫机动队的枪口。而尤利娅则更加谨慎，她只对那些失事偏离着陆区，警卫机动队回收不及的降落舱下手，同时保证那些关键而珍贵的货物不经过自己的手就不能销赃。

自视甚高的尤利娅如何看待她目前的处境呢？她依然保持着集邮的习惯，把自己的车辆涂成联盟航天器类似的颜色，某种意义上也是一种怀念，比起在泥坑中一呼百应，她可能更喜欢邮局职员的生活，更喜欢那个平淡，却有更多途径学习的年代。不过她也很清楚那个时代已经彻底过去——如今的地诺航天已经不再肩负着崇高的理想，所以，她才能毫不犹豫地对无人看守的降落舱吃干抹净。

Just who could possibly keep in check those scavengers—predators as ravenous and indiscriminate as vultures? This was the primary question the Security Bureau, and specifically its Cosmodrome Tactical unit, strove to answer. Yet, infiltrating scavenger organizations proved difficult; within these fluid, shifting collectives lay tightly knit subgroups, each loyal to a leader known only by a nickname. These subgroups used the larger scavenger crowds as cover, appearing in unexpected places and vanishing cunningly, taking only the most valuable cargo and parts—sometimes even entire vehicles. Consequently, the Security Bureau surmised that their ringleader was likely a highly educated individual of Earth origin.

Yulia was one such scavenger leader, known by the nickname "Postman." Yet, contrary to expectations, she was not from Earth; her formal education amounted to nothing more than primary schooling in the Republic. That this seemingly delicate, youthful girl rose to become the most respected scavenger leader was a testament to her genius-level intellect.


Despite lacking a scholarly air, Yulia possessed an extraordinary capacity for learning. Piecing together fragments of information scavenged from various sources, she devised ingenious, practical methods to dismantle and reassemble components, all while discerning the true value of the items she handled. Her weapon of choice—the RPG-7—was a tool demanding expert skill; while most users merely sent its rockets careening wildly like fireworks, Yulia could fire with pinpoint accuracy. She was also uniquely capable of mastering complex hardware like the AGS-17 grenade launcher. Surrounded by simple-minded, shortsighted individuals, Yulia inevitably felt a sense of intellectual superiority.


Her nickname, "Postman," hinted—albeit imperfectly—at her background. She had once been a postal worker, though not a courier who delivered letters on foot. During the Space Race era, the United Territories had launched ambitious agricultural and industrial projects, including an attempt to establish a postal network spanning the known continents of Nox. While remnants of this system persisted only in United Territories settlements and select major cities of the Republic—staffed predominantly by Earth-born personnel—Yulia, like many Nox natives, had once served within that organization. As early as her third year of primary school, Yulia’s teacher had already declared her the most outstanding student they had ever taught. Yet, after completing primary school, she found no openings available at either the Knights' boarding schools or the secondary schools run by the Administration’s Department of Education—all the spots had been pre-emptively claimed by various individuals deemed "qualified." Given her educational background, the most respectable job she could secure was that of a postal clerk in the United Territories.

At the post office, she handled countless letters and postcards. Through the imagery on stamps and cards, she gained glimpses of Earth’s towering mountains and mighty rivers, icy plains and deep canyons, exotic wildlife, and—of course—massive ships, dams, and skyscrapers. Yulia’s innate brilliance shone through: a single glance was often enough for her to deduce the function and operating principles of the complex machinery depicted on the stamps.

The job also gave her ample opportunity to read books and magazines. Beyond the knowledge she gained, these materials revealed that the Union was on its last legs; inevitable downsizing at the post office meant her own livelihood was precarious. Yet, Yulia never anticipated that her career would come to an end before the post office itself did.


During the chaotic period following the collapse, a gang of drifters led by a woman named Bola stormed the post office, intent on robbery. Finding little cash or anything of value left on the premises, Bola seized Yulia as a hostage to demand a ransom from the United Territories—mistaking her for an Earthling because of the uniform cap she wore.

After discovering Yulia’s true identity, Bola attempted to play the part of a mob boss, trying to force her captive to swear allegiance to her as "Godmother" and become a devoted henchman. However, a series of events ensued—events Bola could never quite articulate clearly, and which Yulia disdained to mention—that resulted in a complete reversal of roles: Bola, despite possessing only brute strength, ended up becoming the "Goddaughter," while Yulia transformed from a captive into the gang's leader. Yulia knew full well that brazen, armed robbery or kidnapping would invite the Security Bureau to make an example of the perpetrator; that was why she pioneered the trade of scavenging at landing zones—or, at the very least, was one of its earliest practitioners.

As the United Territories retrenched and the Republic faced turmoil, an influx of unskilled opportunists quickly turned the "blue ocean" market into a cutthroat "red ocean." While scavengers swarmed in droves, Yulia’s standing actually rose. Reckless, starving hotheads would rush headlong into the guns of the Cosmodrome Tactical Unit the moment they spotted the parachutes of landing pods. Yulia, however, was far more prudent; she targeted only those pods that had crashed far off-course—beyond the Cosmodrome Tactical Unit's immediate reach—and ensured that key, high-value cargo could not be fenced without passing through her hands.

How did Yulia, a woman who held herself in high regard, view her current lot in life? She maintained her old habit of stamp collecting and painted her vehicles in colors reminiscent of Union spacecraft—a gesture of nostalgia, in a sense. She might well have preferred the life of a post office clerk—that era of quiet routine yet abundant opportunities for learning—over her current existence, where she commanded a crowd from the mud. Yet, she was keenly aware that such times were gone for good; the Aerospace of today no longer bore the weight of lofty ideals, which was precisely why she could, without a second thought, strip unguarded landing pods of every last scrap of value.
