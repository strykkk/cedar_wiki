"Tsukimi-hime", (lady-Tsukimi) an active user on a firearms bulletin board, comes across as arrogant and overbearing; yet, her real-life persona—Yumiko Nishimura, a high school girl—actually owns a machine gun and knows exactly how to use it. Consequently, when debating firearms online, she can produce the kind of concrete, hands-on expertise that armchair military enthusiasts—who rely solely on theory—simply cannot match. She invariably wins the tedious squabbles over technical details.

lady-Tsukimi’s father is the president of the "Nishimura-gumi," a massive conglomerate with deep roots in construction, advertising, and entertainment. As the president's daughter, she is expected to maintain the image of a refined young lady in public. However, the Nishimura family’s true "core business" is the *yakuza* underworld; their organization traces its lineage back to the Sengoku period, commanding such local authority that even powerful feudal lords of old were obliged to treat them with deference. The townspeople under their protection praise their chivalrous code and the economic benefits brought by their business ventures. The modern-day Nishimura-gumi has moved past the era of constant, violent turf wars; during festivals, they even make the rounds to foster neighborhood goodwill—a duty that forces Yumiko to attend community association meetings, bowing and smiling politely. Since the family's heirloom firearms serve no practical purpose in their legitimate business operations, they have become lady-Tsukimi’s personal playthings. The more she suppresses her true self in daily life, the more intensely she vents that pent-up energy online and at the shooting range.

One eventually grows bored of shooting in a soundproofed underground range. lady-Tsukimi longs to find a shooting paradise free from restrictive laws—a place where she can fire to her heart's content in wide-open wilderness. Upon learning that a hunting TV program is planning to film on the planet Nox, she immediately sees an opportunity: by becoming an investor in the show, she gains a pretext to shoot guns on an alien world while simultaneously escaping the tedious social obligations of her family life. To secure funding from her family, Yumiko Nishimura claimed she would ride the wave of the "Nox-Earth economy"?  sparked by Mariana. She proposed building a "Twin-Planet" ecosystem to empower traditional industries through technological upgrades, laying a solid foundation with accumulated technical expertise to deliver a "dimensional strike" against sunset industries—thereby establishing a reliable second growth curve. Elders, already concerned about the lack of a new generation to take over the family business, praised Yumiko’s innovative spirit and supported her decision to drop out of school and launch her own venture.
