	Elizabeth possessed a slim and graceful figure. Her pale limbs, coupled with a flowing black veil, made her appear like a skeleton shrouded in black mist in the shadows, quite frightening indeed. As a renowned fortune teller, Elizabeth's "divination" took many forms: sometimes she would hold a crystal ball and talk to herself, observe bird tracks by day, gaze at the stars by night and sometimes even interpret omens from the clouds, then confidently tell others that they had encountered some kind of misfortune... In reality, it was all just her charlatanry.

	Elizabeth was extremely sensitive, even to the point of being neurotic, making it difficult for her to communicate normally with others. She constantly observed the behavior and activities of others, even analyzing their micro-expressions. Many behaviors and expressions that seemed perfectly normal to others often touched a nerve with her, which she would repeatedly ponder and analyze from various angles, what was the deeper meaning behind their actions? Were they plotting something to harm her? So, in order to thwart such a plot, she began to fabricate all sorts of ominous signs to deter others.

	However, Elizabeth was not a charlatan; her true divination involved lighting her old oil lamp and observing the smoke. Because of her exceptional skill, many people with various ulterior motives sought her help, which further exacerbated her neuroticism. She preferred dealing with dogs to people, because dogs were always perceived as having simpler minds.

	Elizabeth was wary of anything unfamiliar, especially the bizarre electronic gadgets from Earth that made crackling noises and flashed various lights, sending chills down her spine. She stubbornly believed they were used to control people's minds and therefore resolutely stayed away from all electronic products.

	Elizabeth never revealed the reason for her neuroticism, she was a victim of the Entente "mind control" research during the Cold War. At the time, the Entente side believed that the Union possessed some kind of "brainwashing" technology, which was why they had won the loyalty of many locals in Nox and even the Entente elites. Therefore, they began "counter-revolutionary" research into mind control, consciousness distortion, and memory implantation, hoping to cultivate their own loyalists in the Zarab region. Elizabeth was unfortunately captured and became a test subject. The "mind control" research unsurprisingly failed and the Entente turned to more traditional methods to cultivate agents, including Mazdak. However, the damage suffered by the "test subjects" was already deeply etched into their memories…

	Pola proclaims herself the second-in-command of the gang known as "The Postmen", though this claim is only acknowledged by others when she is present. Following scavenger custom, Pola required a Codename. After much mental gymnastics, she settled upon "Big Tiger" and insisted others use it, only to forget the name entirely after a night's sleep, revealing the poverty of her imagination. Others typically refer to her as "Hungry Stick".

	Though Pola occasionally puts on airs as second-in-command, It is merely a case of playing dress-up—an imitation of Yulia. At heart, her outlook on life is as simple as that of an unweaned infant; her greatest desire is to eat—preferably meat. When well-fed, Pola is carefree, easy to get along with, and even eager to help others. However, if hunger strikes, she begins to wail loudly, becoming irritable and aggressive. If the hunger persists, she starts devising various schemes to get food using her not-so-bright mind—plans that, once put into action, almost invariably end in disaster. And if she goes hungry any longer, she wilts completely, collapsing onto the ground, unable to move until someone feeds her.

	Pola's brute strength and the sharp axe in her grasp form the most palpable military deterrent within the "The Postman" gang. Those ruthless individuals seeking to double-cross their own kind fear first and foremost her unstoppable destructive power, only then the "The Postman's" cunning intellect. Pola can haul heavy wreckage and bend thick steel plates with her bare hands. Such strength transcends mortal limits, understood only as a self-taught EGO technique.

	When and how did Pola acquire such skills? Pola harbours no desire to conceal her early years, though she herself cannot recall them. What is known is this: Pola once roamed the borderlands between the United Territories and the Republic, taken in by many households, likely because they expected her to work, yet driven out time and again, probably due to her insatiable appetite. Consequently, Pola received little formal education, she cannot read or write, nor can she count properly.

	Pola's life story began with a robbery, incited by a gang of thugs, she raided a post office and abducted Yulia, mistaking her for an Earthling. Yet Yulia soon turned the tables, becoming the gang's leader, while Pola acknowledged her as her "godmother". Now, Pola serves as Yulia's foremost tool. Beyond lending her strength in fights and labour, she's also tasked with testing any potentially dangerous homemade contraptions. Ever eager for a bite to eat, Bola remains blissfully oblivious to danger. Beyond exploiting her, Yulia appears to harbour genuine sympathy for her unfortunate situation.
