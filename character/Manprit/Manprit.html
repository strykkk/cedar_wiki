Personal History

       Jacqueline, a top student, was well-versed in the Entent advancements in military technologiy of recent years and was eager to apply these technologies to her confrontation with the warrant officer. However, Jacqueline's high-tech solutions required substantial material support, which meant she had to rely on Manprit, the diligent and resourceful logistics contractor within the Multinational force Ruvila.

       Manprit always wore a businessman's obsequious smile, readily agreeing to whatever Jacqueline asked. Her connections truly put even the most legitimate logistical channels to shame. Whether it was strictly controlled military supplies—the latest bulletproof plates, encrypted radios, and armor-piercing incendiary bombs—or scarce luxury goods—champagne and truffles, designer perfumes, and exquisite porcelain—she could guarantee to buy and ship them. She even managed to procure prototypes not yet officially included in the procurement list. As a "pure businesswoman," Manprit was very grateful to the warrant officer for causing the mission so many losses, which greatly increased her revenue.

       If it weren't for her ability to handle a carbine with such skill, no one would guess she was once a well-trained air assault officer, and even Jacqueline's classmate at the military academy strugled. Thanks to Manprit's care, Jacqueline continued to live a life of luxury on the battlefield, while the supplier herself lived a life of almost unbelievable simplicity—she spent her nights in her truck guarding her goods, and her meals consisted of flatbread and chickpea or lentil curry paste spread on an old tablecloth, the color and texture of which were unbearable for the other members of the mission to look at. Therefore, everyone believed that this diligent and frugal supplier's long journey to Nox was partly out of consideration for her classmate relationship with Jacqueline.

       Of course, their judgment was completely wrong. Jacqueline, who had grown up in luxury, was oblivious to the cost of living. Her habit was to place orders without asking prices, as long as the goods were of high quality. This gave Manprit room to manipulate the system and inflate prices. On her invoices, a stainless steel coffee pot might cost 60,000 Vespuccian-Pound, a disposable napkin 10,000 Vespuccian-Pound, and advanced equipment that was worth the price was exorbitantly expensive.

Personal Report

       Manprit is a diligent and resourceful logistics contractor. She provides extensive material support for the RuviLa Mission's operations on Nox, while sincerely hoping that this support will last forever.

       Manprit was born into a poor immigrant family in Kalistan . Unable to afford expensive private universities, she chose to receive higher education through military academy. Her success was not only due to her aptitude for test-taking skills, but also thanks to the diversity enhancement program vigorously promoted by Lieutenant General McDonald.

       During her service in the Vespuccian Army, she was an outstanding air assault officer, but retired quickly after meeting the minimum service requirement. She then used her connections with the Lt.Gen. McDonald to enter a Private Military Company ,and accidentally, she also provided protection for Lt.Gen. McDonald's daughter, Jacqueline, in various operations.

       Jacqueline, pampered since childhood, was oblivious to the cost of living, always placing orders without asking prices. This gave Manprit room to embezzle. On her invoices, a stainless steel coffee pot might cost as much as 60,000 pounds, a disposable napkin 10,000 pounds, and the logistics costs of shipping these supplies from Earth to Nox were even more staggering. Despite her skillful in enriching herself, the supplier lived a remarkably frugal life—she spent her nights in her truck guarding her goods, eating only flatbread spread on an old tablecloth, topped with chickpea or lentil curry paste, the color and texture of which were unbearable for the other members of the mission.

       Incredibly, Manprit spent most of the money she earned from the Ministry of Defense subsidizing her fellow countrymen to circulate in The United Vespuccian States through various channels. Her family escaped their misery thanks to the help of their fellow villagers, so from a young age she firmly believed that she should work hard to earn money and help her country folks also run to The united State of Vespucci to earn big bucks in the land full of dummy

       In Manprit's eyes, Jacqueline and the warrant officer were both her benefactors and saviors of her fellow villagers. She hoped Jacqueline would forever chase after the warrant officer in Nox, and the warrant officer would forever keep making small, back-and-forth moves on Jacqueline, creating a constant back-and-forth and ensuring her business would prosper endlessly, while her fellow villagers could continue to enjoy The United Vespuccian States.

       They want to get rid of Bharat's discriminatory policies as soon as possible.
